## SECTION 3 — THE PLATFORM (the 4 pillars)

Heading: **"Four engines. One pipeline. Zero spreadsheets."**

### Discovery
- **B2B databases** — millions of companies indexed by industry, headcount, location, and intent
- **Local business intelligence** — boutique hospitality, restaurants, marinas, event venues
- **Social discovery** — bio-parsed contact info from Instagram and LinkedIn
- **Direct website intelligence** — paste a URL, we extract everything the public site reveals
- All four feed one deduplicated table — same business across multiple sources becomes one record, not four

### Enrichment
- Verified business emails via professional enrichment APIs
- Phone numbers, contact titles, decision-maker mapping
- Website analysis — tech stack, social presence, SEO signals
- Industry-aware tagging — restaurants get one workflow, agencies another, hotels another
- All running automatically on a schedule. No buttons to press.

### Intelligence
- **AI scoring** — every lead gets a Hot / Warm / Cool fit score with the model's reasoning
- **Strategic audit** — a full marketing diagnostic of the prospect's business, generated in minutes
- **Opportunity surfacing** — the platform tells you which leads to work first and why
- **Pipeline you can trust** — cross-source deduplication, full activity history, soft-delete archive

### Activation
- AI-drafted outreach — LinkedIn messages, cold email, follow-up sequences
- AI-generated content — social posts, ad hooks, scripts, captions
- Multi-pipeline routing — same prospect can serve multiple business lines simultaneously
- Built-in segmentation, scheduling, and approval workflow

## SECTION 5 — HOW IT WORKS

Heading: **"From kickoff to first qualified lead in 5 business days."**

**Day 1 — Onboarding.**
Define your ICP. Upload your existing customer list (so we can pattern-match), your existing lead list (so we don't re-pitch), and your brand voice samples. The platform learns who you sell to.

**Days 2–4 — Discovery + Enrichment.**
The platform runs your ICP across all four discovery sources. New leads are enriched with contact data, scored for fit, and audited. You watch the pipeline fill in real time.

**Day 5 — First Outreach Wave.**
You approve the top-scored leads, review the AI-drafted messages, and the platform ships your first sequence. From there it runs continuously, with new leads landing daily and audits running on demand.

## TIER-LEVEL COST PASS-THROUGH MATH (internal, for pricing defense)

*This section is internal — don't put it on the public site. It exists so the team can defend pricing if a prospect pushes back.*

**Platform cost per month at ~10 active clients (across all tiers):**
- Hunter (Growth plan, 2,500 verifications): $99
- Apollo (Professional): $99
- Clay (Starter trial → first paid tier): $149
- Apify (modest paid): $20
- PhantomBuster (Starter): $59
- Instantly: $97
- OpenAI: $50
- Grok: $20
- Google Places: $0 (free tier credit)
- Netlify + HostGator: $15
- **Total platform OpEx: ~$608/month**

**Revenue at conservative client mix (10 clients):**
- 6 Starter × $497 = $2,982
- 3 Growth × $1,497 = $4,491
- 1 Scale × $3,997 = $3,997
- **MRR: $11,470**

**Gross margin: $11,470 - $608 = $10,862 (~95% gross margin)**

This is the unit economics that make the platform fundable AND fundable-without-funding. The expensive tools (Clay, PhantomBuster, Apollo Pro) are recovered by client #2.

## WHAT CHANGED FROM v2

1. **Removed every founder-name reference.** "Talk to Val" became "Talk to our team." "I built" became "We built." All copy now reads as a *company*, not a person. Larger-than-life means plural.

2. **Pricing tiers added.** Four tiers — Starter / Growth / Scale / Enterprise — with explicit feature lists at each level. Cost-passthrough math added as internal-only section.

3. **Hero replaced with the mission sentence.** v2 opened with "AI-Powered Marketing Intelligence." v3 opens with the buyer-side promise: *power up, take back time, increase revenue, on automation*. Better fit for non-technical buyers.

4. **"For agencies" section is now a real pitch, not a footnote.** White-label is a core Scale/Enterprise tier driver. v3 sells it explicitly.

5. **Added content generation as a top-line pillar.** v2 mentioned it; v3 sells it. Daily AI social posts are the most concrete "freedom of automation" feature for buyers to imagine.

6. **Removed locality language entirely.** Already done in v2. Reconfirmed in v3.

7. **Roadmap section dropped from public copy.** v2 had it. v3 doesn't — instead, future features are listed as deliverables of higher tiers (AI commercial generation = Scale tier). This converts roadmap into pricing leverage instead of "things we don't have yet."

8. **Trust section structured for when proof exists.** Placeholder targets given so the team knows what numbers to populate as they appear.
