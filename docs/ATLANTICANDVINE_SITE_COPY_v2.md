## SECTION 1 — WHAT IT DOES (the 4 pillars)

Heading: **"Four sources. One engine. Zero spreadsheets."**

Intro line: *Every prospect that lands in your pipeline came through one of these four channels. Cross-source dedup means no duplicates. Auto-segmentation means each lead is tagged for the right pipeline. AI scoring means you know where to start every morning.*

### Pillar 1 — Multi-source lead discovery
- Apollo for B2B contacts by ICP
- Google Places for hospitality and local businesses
- Instagram for boutique businesses that live on social
- Direct contact-page scraping for anything in between

All four feed one dedup'd database. A restaurant found by Google Places and your Apollo search becomes one lead, not two. Same business, all sources, one row.

### Pillar 2 — Automated enrichment
- Hunter.io for verified business emails
- On-page scraping for contact info Hunter can't find
- Instagram bio parsing for boutique businesses that aren't in any B2B database
- All enrichment runs daily on a schedule — no buttons to push, no spreadsheets to maintain

### Pillar 3 — AI scoring + AI audits
- Every lead gets a Hot / Warm / Cool fit score with the AI's reasoning
- Every lead can trigger a full Strategic Marketing Audit — generated automatically, delivered in minutes
- Both run on top of the enrichment layer, so the AI works from real data, not guesses

### Pillar 4 — Pipeline you can actually trust
- Business segmentation (AV / EBW / Both) so leads route to the right pipeline automatically
- Cross-source dedup by normalized domain
- Soft-delete archive so you can clean up without fear
- Activity log for every change — nothing disappears

## SECTION 2 — WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

Heading: **"Not a stack. A system."**

Intro: *You've probably already paid for Apollo, Hunter, Clay, Hubspot, ChatGPT, and a dozen Zapier flows to glue them together. Each one does part of the job. None of them know what the others are doing. Atlantic & Vine is built different.*

Three differentiators, side-by-side:

**One source of truth.**
Every lead lives in one database. Every source — Apollo, Places, Instagram, your CSV imports, your audit form — writes to the same row when the domain matches. No more "wait, which tool has the latest version?"

**AI throughout, not bolted on.**
Most "AI tools" are a GPT wrapper on top of a 2018 CRM. Atlantic & Vine was designed AI-first: scoring, audits, enrichment, and outreach all run through the same intelligence layer, working from the same data, on the same business context.

**Built for multi-tenant from day one.**
Most marketing tools force you into their workspace structure. Atlantic & Vine ships with tenant isolation built in — agency-ready out of the box, so the same platform that runs Atlantic & Vine internally is the platform we deploy to clients.

## SECTION 3 — HOW IT WORKS (3-step explainer)

Heading: **"From URL to pipeline in 90 seconds."**

**Step 1 — Tell us who you sell to.**
Pick from preset ICPs (boutique hotels, charter operators, wedding planners, restaurants, agencies) or define your own. ICP gets saved to your profile and reused across every campaign.

**Step 2 — We find the leads.**
The system runs your ICP across four sources simultaneously. New leads land in your dashboard, deduplicated, scored, and segmented automatically. No CSV exports. No "first download the contacts, then upload to the enrichment tool."

**Step 3 — We tell you what to say.**
Every lead gets an AI score with reasoning. High-fit leads trigger a full strategic audit. You see the why, the gap, the angle — and a draft of the first message you'd send. Approve, edit, send.

## WHAT CHANGED FROM v1

1. **Dropped all locality copy.** No "USVI specialists" or "St. Croix-based" language. The product works everywhere; v1 underplayed reach.

2. **Replaced "LinkedIn-focused" / "LinkedIn-first" wording with "across all your networks" or "multi-source intelligence."** v1 was tied to LinkedIn; v2 reflects what we actually do (Apollo + Places + IG + scrape).

3. **Softened or removed unverifiable specific stats.** Replaced "300% increase in pipeline" type claims with concrete capability descriptions ("Hot/Warm/Cool fit score on every lead").

4. **Lead with vision, not features.** v1 was "we do X, Y, Z." v2 is "here's the new category — AI-powered marketing intelligence — and here's how we built it."

5. **Honest roadmap section.** v1 implied everything works today. v2 separates what's live (discovery, enrichment, scoring, audits, pipeline) from what's coming (commercial generation, outbound automation, white-label). This builds trust instead of eroding it.

6. **Reframed the audit CTA.** v1: "Get a free audit." v2: "Paste your domain. Get back a strategic audit, an ICP, and three real prospects." Specific, verifiable, demonstrates capability.

7. **Added "For agencies" angle.** v1 was service-business only. The master architecture doc says white-label is a core product direction; v2 reflects that.

8. **Confident tone, no apology.** v1 hedged on capability. v2 states clearly what the platform does and what's coming. The work backs it up.
